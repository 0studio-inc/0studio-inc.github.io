# Motivation

"News is to the mind what sugar is to the body"

R.Dobelli 2010 Avoid News: Towards a Healthy News Diet (opens new window)

We need help with digesting the gargantuan firehose of news that we stare at every day. There is a mountain of great value on the Internet. The best articles I find, I read many times over. I like to go back to them to refresh my memory if something does not make sense.

# Problem

Staying on top of what you want or need to know can be a challenge. Machine Learning and Data Science are two examples of fields that produce overwhelming amounts of new technologies, and tools that some of us read about every day. The difficulty is that we don't retain or follow up on enough of these valuable materials that we have luckily came across on the bottomless Internet.

Forgetting important content and loosing valuable websites. These can be some tutorials, lectures, articles that explained something really well but when after a while you wanted to find that resource again, it was impossible. They come up in conversations: you want to share a website you found 2 weeks ago, and now can't find it in your history anywhere. Oh, it was a different device, perhaps company laptop.

# Solutions

People recognize this problem and try to solve it.

buildingasecondbrain.com

Building a Second Brain (opens new window) is a course teaching how to organize your knowledge and increase creativity using a few tools. One of fundamental ideas there is the "Caputre, Organize, Distill, Express" process.

# Environment

"The most powerful catalyst for building better habits is environment design (what some researchers call choice architecture). The idea is simple: the environments where we live and work influence our behaviors .."

Chemistry Habits (opens new window)

We want to minimize the activation energy to get to articles that are valuable to us. They need to be easily available so they win the race to fill our attention when we need a break.

# MindSync

We allow you to capture, organize, and distill of all of the web content you get exposed to.

As per Dobelli's advice, perhaps to "avoid news" is too difficult for some of us. We habitually browse news-feeds when we need a pause or when we have a spare moment. Taking that away is infeasible; we do need these pauses.

However, if we "replace news" with healthier pieces of information, then we transform a bad habit into a good one while keeping our habit nearly unchanged. Our pauses are now expected to offer value that matters to us. Stress level is expected to go lower.

When we mindsync, our attention is on the things it should be on. It is designed to display content and eliminate unnecessary distractions.

MindSync is the place where you want to store all the websites you think you may ever like to read again. That becomes the base of your focused content, which you can expand on for years.

A secure library that individuals can trust and completely control. Ownership of all data, preferences, metadata must be on the users side. If user chooses to, they can directly monetize their preferences.

The user can leave the platform at any time and retain access to all of their data. If they decide to return in a decade, they should be able to do that and leave again if they wish to with highest convenience. Users are our customers, and we do everything to accomodate their needs first.

If we get to spend more time at reading valuable materials with understanding, then the complex ideas on paper will start to percolate into our lives and we will gain the ability to solve more problems. As we advance our knowledge, we impact our community.
